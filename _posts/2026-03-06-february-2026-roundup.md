## Articles & Learning

- [**Explore the Stratosphere with a DIY Pico Balloon | IEEE Spectrum**](https://spectrum.ieee.org/explore-stratosphere-diy-pico-balloon)<br>
  High-altitude balloon with a Raspberry Pi Pico. － Noah

- [**The Hidden Costs of Using Zephyr (and How to Mitigate Them) | Shawn Hymel**](https://shawnhymel.com/3193/the-hidden-costs-of-using-zephyr-and-how-to-mitigate-them/)<br>
  A look at the less obvious costs of adopting Zephyr, and strategies to
  mitigate them.
  [Discussed on the Zephyr podcast](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=X_vJWP2pirI)
  as well! － Noah

- [**Building Secure OTA Updates for ESP32 Over BLE with Rust**](https://gill.net.in/posts/building-secure-ota-updates-for-esp32-over-ble-with-rust/)<br>
  Walkthrough of building secure OTA updates for ESP32 over BLE using
  Rust. － Noah

- [**MicroPython Now Requires AI Disclosure on Every Pull Request | Adafruit Blog**](https://blog.adafruit.com/2026/02/19/micropython-now-requires-ai-disclosure-on-every-pull-request/)<br>
  MicroPython now requires contributors to disclose AI usage on every pull
  request. － Noah

- [**Unlocking 15% More Performance: A Case Study in LLVM Optimization for RISC-V | Igalia**](https://blogs.igalia.com/compilers/2025/11/22/unlocking-15-more-performance-a-case-study-in-llvm-optimization-for-risc-v/)<br>
  LLVM work on RISC-V continues with lots of improvements over the last few
  months; here is a 15% performance improvement case study. － Noah

- [**How to Learn Embedded Rust for Beginners | ImplRust**](https://blog.implrust.com/posts/2025/12/how-to-learn-embedded-rust/)<br>
  A beginner’s guide to getting started with embedded Rust. － Noah

- [**OTA Update Engines Compared | Rugix Blog**](https://rugix.org/blog/2026-02-28-ota-update-engines-compared/)<br>
  Comparison of OTA update engines by Maxi, a Interrupt contributor and
  community member.

- [**Serial EERAM | Microchip**](https://www.microchip.com/en-us/products/memory/serial-eeram)<br>
  Interesting chip, from a project shared on
  [Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46947096): EERAM,
  EEPROM-backed SRAM, which automatically saves/restores on power loss. － Noah
